Sebastian Bordt

I'm a recently graduated Computer Science PhD student, supervised by Ulrike von Luxburg at the University of Tübingen. This summer, I interned with Rich Caruana at Microsoft Research. I currently work as a postdoctoral reseracher at the University of Tübingen.

Most of my work is in interpretable machine learning. For example, I haved worked on the connections between post-hoc methods and interpretable models, and on the suitability of explanation algorithms for regulation. If you are interested in these topic, take a look at our blog post. In a different line of work, we ask when salincy maps for image classifiers are perceptually aligned, and how this is connected with robust models.

Recently, I have become interested in Large Language Models. During my internship at Microsoft Research, we studied memorization and learning of tabular data in LLMs. We also did some experiments with GPT-4 and GAMs in a healthcare setting. In our undergraduate exam on algorithms in Tübingen this spring, we found that GPT-4 performed on par with our students.

Prior to my work in machine learning, I obtained Master's degrees in Mathematics and Economics at TUM and LMU in Munich. I also spent some time at the Munich Graduate School of Economics.

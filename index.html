Sebastian Bordt

I am a PhD Student at the Theory of Machine Learning Group at the University of T√ºbingen, supervised by Ulrike von Luxburg. I'm broadly interested in the analysis of machine learning algorithms and their societal impact. My current work focuses on explainable machine learning. For example, I haved worked on the connections between post-hoc methods and interpretable models, and on the suitability of explanation methods for regulation. If you are interested in these topic, take a look at our blog post. In a different line of work, we ask when salincy maps for image classifiers are perceptually aligned, and how this is connected with robust models. I have also done some early experiments with ChatGPT.

Prior to my work in machine learning, I obtained Master's degrees in Mathematics and Economics at TUM and LMU in Munich. I also spent some time at the Munich Graduate School of Economics.
